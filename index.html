Transforming Data into Action

Bridging complex information with clear, impactful communication across environmental, agricultural, and health sectors.

I work with governments, international organisations and companies to transform environmental, agricultural and health data into tools people actually use. Through advanced data analytics, I structure, analyse and visualise complex information so that what matters is clear, comparable and actionable.

I design dashboards, analytical frameworks, fact sheets and reporting tools that support monitoring, compliance, investment decisions and sustainability strategies.

But if data informs, great communication moves. I use evidence to shape narratives, challenge assumptions and inspire action. I design communication products and campaigns that speak to their audience, whether that is a boardroom, a policy team or a remote rural community.

With over ten years of experience across Africa and Europe, I bring a global perspective and deep sectoral knowledge — a genuine 360° view that goes well beyond the buzzword.

Data does not change the world.
People do.

A People-Centred Approach

Despite having a strong technical background, my work has never been driven by technical considerations alone. I graduated in biotechnology, later specialised in agriculture and livestock production, and I am currently completing a PhD in agriculture. Along the way, I also undertook a postdoctoral research fellowship in Japan focused on the application of data science in agricultural systems.

Early in my career, particularly through my work in Sub-Saharan Africa, my understanding of science, communication and project management shifted profoundly. I learned that even the most robust technical solutions — and the most accurate data — do not create change unless they are culturally relevant and grounded in context, language and lived experience.

This realisation shapes how I work. I listen first, learn deeply, and only then build tools, narratives and solutions that respect the people and environments they are meant to serve. It might sound simple, but learning to listen without prejudice has been one of the hardest and most valuable lessons of my career.

Language is central to this approach: having trained, worked and studied across multiple countries and languages, I work professionally across Spanish, English, Italian, Portuguese, French and German, with additional experience communicating in other languages in field and intercultural settings. Being a polyglot is not simply a technical asset, but a way of building trust, understanding nuance, and producing culturally grounded, relevant work.

Whether working with communities in remote regions, speaking at international forums, or collaborating with policymakers and practitioners, I bring not only technical expertise, but a genuinely people-centred lens shaped by years of cross-cultural engagement and linguistic fluency.
