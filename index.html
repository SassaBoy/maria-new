About Me

Scientific Communicator
& Data Analyst

I graduated in biotechnology, later specialised in agriculture and livestock production, and I am currently completing a PhD in agriculture. Despite having a strong technical background, my work has never been driven by technical considerations alone.

Early in my career, particularly through my work in Sub-Saharan Africa, my understanding of science, communication and project management shifted profoundly. I learned that even the most robust technical solutions — and the most accurate data — do not create change unless they are culturally relevant and grounded in context, language and lived experience.

I listen first, learn deeply, and only then build tools, narratives and solutions that respect the people and environments they are meant to serve. Language is central to this approach: I work professionally across Spanish, English, Italian, Portuguese, French and German, with additional experience communicating in other languages in field and intercultural settings.

Whether working with communities in remote regions, speaking at international forums, or collaborating with policymakers and practitioners, I bring not only technical expertise, but a genuinely people-centred lens shaped by years of cross-cultural engagement.

Putting Data to Use

Data informs

I work with governments, international organisations, and companies to analyse environmental, agricultural, and health data and turn it into tools that are actually used.

Through data analytics, I structure, analyse, and visualise complex information so that priorities are clear, comparable, and usable for decision-making, reporting, and accountability.

This includes the design of dashboards, analytical frameworks, fact sheets, and reporting tools that support monitoring, compliance, investment decisions, and sustainability strategies.

Communication enables use

Alongside analytical work, I design communication outputs that help people understand and engage with technical evidence. This includes short videos, visual explainers, brochures, campaigns, and written content developed from the same data and analysis.

Communication work focuses on clarity, accuracy, and audience fit. Materials are adapted for different contexts, whether for policy teams, technical partners, decision-makers, or community-level initiatives, without oversimplifying the underlying evidence.

Data alone does not create change.
People do.
